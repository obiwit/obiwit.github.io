More Resources on Machine Learning

By Beatriz Borges R, posted on Jan 26, 12018

If you want to learn more about Machine Learning, the good news is there are plenty of resources to get you learning out there! The bad news is there really are plenty of resources out there!

Here's what I'd recommend doing:

In terms of Online Courses, Videos and MOOCs

Check out Stanford/Andrew Ng's Coursera course. It gives a great overview on Machine Learning. After that, Andrew Ng has also recently done a specialization on Neural Networks and Deep Learning, also on Coursera, so that's something you also pursue ;)

In Terms of Books

In terms of books, in reading order, I'd recommend:

  1. Fundamentals of Machine Learning for Predictive Data Analysis, for a fantastic overview of Supervised Learning Algorithms and a detailed explanation of a process you can follow whenever you need to use Machine Learning (CRISP-DM) [unfortunately, there is no free online version of the book],
  2. Introduction to Statistical Learning using R (ISL), and/or Deep Learning, depending on what interests you more [both books are freely available online, at their respective websites],
  3. By now you probably know which book(s) you want to read next way better than I ever could predict! :D

In terms of Other Platforms

Check out kaggle! It's a really great platform, where you can get your feet wet doing machine learning on real datasets, and you're sure to learn tons of practical knowledge there, both implementing solutions and reading the discussion forums!

TL; DR

If you are still overwhelmed by these recommendations and want only one resource - bearing in mind that, at least in the beginning, you will probably miss out on several concepts - I'd have to say kaggle. Between the real datasets, the great community, the guides for beginners, and how you'll gain actual experience in Data Science and Machine Learning, kaggle is very hard to top!
