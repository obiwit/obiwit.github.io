Why Machine Learning is Cool

By Beatriz Borges R, edited on Mar 25, 12018

Kurt Vonnegut, one of my favorite writers, described science as “magic that works.”

And indeed, when I first learned to code, there was something magical about it. How you could compute a result that wasn’t there before. From nothing, just your own mental conception of that process. But unfortunately, as time goes by, it’s something you grow accustomed to. And then I discovered Machine Learning - and fell in love again. Machine Learning’s magic is of a stronger kind - you are not calculating a result you didn’t know before, but gaining knowledge you didn’t know (and maybe weren’t even expecting) before. And that’s awesome.

I am fascinated by the human ingenuity that permeates the field of Machine Learning. This may sound odd since we don’t usually think of machine learning and artificial intelligence as very human fields. However, I can’t help but feel inspired whenever I learn a new machine learning algorithm. I love how diverse and far-reaching machine learning algorithms are.

And finally, potential. Machine Learning’s potential (and potential payoff) is huge – at the moment, the sky’s the limit, and the time needed to research the next breakthrough seems to be our biggest obstacle. Many Machine Learning problems are limited either by our current algorithms, data or available computing power. But who knows what this will be like in ten years’ time?

We’re also at a time where there isn’t a big barrier to enter this field – you can start now, and catch up relatively easily, as long as you’re willing to put in the work.

This is a very exciting time in AI and Machine Learning, and it’s where you can literally invent the future! What more can one ask?
